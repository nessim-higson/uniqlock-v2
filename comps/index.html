An invisible 6×6 grid; the archive arrives and leaves it in changing compositions — full-bleed, pillars, triptychs, floating chips — every entrance and exit wiping on the metronome, dancers interrupting, the UNIQLOCK clock visiting to remind you who conducts. COMP 06
Z-tunnel broadcast
The broadcast in depth: compositions of butted, aspect-true panels hang as stations down a tunnel; the camera walks the beat; the clock and the dancers float among the work; the lens narrates each approach. Deterministic — same minute, same tunnel, for everyone.
COMP 08
IAAH channel
A television station, not a website. Morning is sparse and white, primetime is dense, 4am is a test card with a tone, idents between the shows. The whole composition breathes on the beat. The schedule is the tempo system. Keys 0/1/2/3 preview blocks, L returns to the live schedule.
COMP 10
Z-rush broadcast
The rush, finally whole: the archive flies at the camera on a beat-stepped conveyor — a new image arriving every two beats — through the recovered lens glass, with the clock and the dancers in the stream. Deterministic, like everything now. (Split-flap and quilt are killed; they rest in versions/r09.)
COMP 12
Swipe
The original UNIQLOCK mechanic turned on the archive: the time small and centered, a different work swiping in from a new direction on every beat. The clock is the host; the work is the wipe.
COMP 11
The dial
Twenty works ring the clock face; the stepped second hand strikes one every three seconds and it floods the background through the displacement shader. A new ring every minute. The radial clock, with the archive as its numerals.
